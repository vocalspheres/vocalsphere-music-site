About Our Library

Free Music Library is a curated collection of royalty‑free tracks for content creators, game developers and storytellers. Inspired by the ethos of NoCopyrightSounds, we aim to empower anyone to enhance their work without worrying about copyright strikes or licensing fees. All our music is composed by independent artists and released under licences that permit reuse in personal and commercial projects.

Our mission

We believe that access to music should be universal. Whether you're producing a video blog, building a mobile game or preparing a classroom presentation, our library offers a variety of styles and moods. We also strive to make our website inclusive by following the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG).

Accessibility features

  • High contrast design: Text on our dark background meets the WCAG requirement of a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text【33227124982459†L798-L805】. This improves readability for visitors with low vision or those using mobile devices in bright environments.
  • Descriptive alternative text: All images include alt text that conveys the essential meaning of the graphic, as recommended by Section 508 guidelines【601546789640432†L74-L89】. Alt text is kept concise and avoids repeating information already present in the surrounding text【601546789640432†L124-L131】.
  • Accessible audio: Our tracks are accompanied by transcripts so that users who are Deaf, hard of hearing or who process information better through reading can access the content. W3C's media accessibility guidance notes that descriptive transcripts are essential for audio‑only content【333096232308326†L120-L139】.
  • Form labels and instructions: All form controls on our contact page are associated explicitly with their labels using the <label> element, following the W3C tutorial on labeling controls【961974359337787†L96-L129】. This association helps assistive technologies relay the purpose of each input field to users.
  • Keyboard‑friendly navigation: The primary navigation and interactive elements such as the collapsible transcripts and mobile menu can be reached and operated using keyboard alone. Focus states are clearly indicated to ensure that users who rely on keyboard or switch devices can see which element is currently active.
